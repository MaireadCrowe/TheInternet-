Enterprise Software

There are various software applications used in different business models to assist with the running of a business. We will look at the following – CRM Systems, SCM Systems, Payment Systems and CMS Systems.

CRM Systems, this stands for Customer Relationship Management Systems.

CRM is a system for managing a company’s interactions with present and potential customers.  It involves using technology to organise and automate sales, marketing, customer service and technical support. CRM allows businesses to have an automated relationship with customers. Now with cloud CRM, users have instant access to information, allowing employees to keep updated wherever they are working from. Examples of CRM systems would be Salesforce, Sage or Microsoft Dynamics 365.

SCM Systems, supply chain management systems.

These systems monitor the oversight of materials, information and finances as they move through the business. Supplier to Manufacturer to Wholesaler to Retailer to Consumer. The system also coordinates and integrates this flow system both within and among other companies. For supply chain management to be effective, the just in time model is the goal of a business. This means stock/product is available when needed. The flow system within a business is divided as follows – The product flow, the information flow and the finances flow. Benefits of a SCM system would be increased efficiency, reduced costs and the ability to monitor trends.

Payment Systems.

Most business now have an online payment system – cash is no longer the popular payment type.
Credit cards are the most popular way to make payments online and in order for a business to facilitate this they require and internet merchant account. This is done through your local banks but there is also a facility to do this through a third-party merchant account provider such as Beanstream. It is quick to set up and no security deposits are required. Often this service is included with ecommerce service packages. The downside of the third-party service is that there are higher fees to be paid. Along with an internet merchant account, the business will also require an internet gateway service. This gateway verifies information, transfer requests and authorises payment transactions in real time.
Paypal, Square and Stripe are companies that provide and all in one payment solution which does not require a merchant account.

CMS Systems - Content Management System.

This is system which organised and facilitates creation of documents and other content. It is mainly used to manage websites and web content. It allows users to add or edit content on a website and there is multiple CMS business facilitation this such as Joomla and Mambo. While these websites offer a good service, they would be aimed at smaller companies. The larger companies would have their own in-house system designed specifically for their business which can be very expensive. This does allow for more consistency on the website.
